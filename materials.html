Materials & Construction Overview

The foundation of the project is defined by the strategic use of high-strength, lightweight alloys and composites. Selection prioritizes a high strength-to-weight ratio and fatigue resistance in critical zones.

Chassis & Structure

The primary load-bearing structure utilizes chromoly steel (4130) for its weldability and tensile strength. For non-structural components and brackets, T6-6061 aluminum is preferred for weight savings. Key fasteners are aircraft-grade titanium alloy where appropriate.

Body Panels & Composites

All non-stressed body panels (e.g., hood, fenders, roof skin) are fabricated from 2x2 twill pre-preg carbon fiber. This achieves the lowest possible weight while maintaining surface finish integrity.
