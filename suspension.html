Suspension & Chassis Geometry

The suspension system is a critical link between the chassis and the road surface. Design objectives focus on controlling wheel motion, managing load transfer, and optimizing tire contact patch.

Kinematic Design: Double Wishbone

A fully adjustable double wishbone setup is utilized at all four corners. The geometry is tuned to minimize camber change during bump and rebound, maintaining a nearly constant instantaneous center of rotation to control roll center height.

Springs, Dampers, and Anti-Roll

Three-way adjustable coil-over dampers are specified, allowing independent control over low-speed compression, high-speed compression, and rebound. Spring rates are 14 kg/mm front and 12 kg/mm rear. The anti-roll bars are adjustable to fine-tune the vehicle's yaw response.
