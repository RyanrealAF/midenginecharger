Cooling & Thermal Management

Effective thermal management is crucial for maintaining engine longevity and performance stability, particularly under high-load conditions. This system addresses coolant, oil, and charged air temperatures.

Fluid Cooling Strategy

A dual-pass radiator configuration is used, maximized to fill the available frontal area. Engine coolant flow is regulated by an electric water pump for precise temperature control, targeting an optimal operating range of 90°C to 95°C.

Oil and Charge-Air Heat Exchangers

Dedicated plate-and-fin oil coolers are mounted in a less turbulent zone for efficient heat rejection. A large, air-to-air intercooler with a bar-and-plate core design is specified to maintain intake air temperatures (IAT) below 45°C at maximum boost.
