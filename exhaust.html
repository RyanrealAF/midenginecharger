Exhaust System Design & Tuning

The exhaust system is designed to maximize gas velocity and scavenging, which directly impacts engine volumetric efficiency and turbocharger spool time. Material selection is based on heat and corrosion resistance.

Header and Downpipe Geometry

The exhaust manifold uses an equal-length, 4-into-1 collector design, constructed from T304 stainless steel. Primary runner diameter is 1.75 inches, and the secondary/downpipe is 3.5 inches. This geometry is optimized for peak torque delivery in the 4500 rpm to 6500 rpm range.

Acoustics and Flow Control

A high-flow catalytic converter is positioned post-turbocharger. A valved muffler system allows for dynamic adjustment of back pressure and acoustic signature, controlled by the ECU based on throttle position and engine load.
